üîç Concepts Explored

  • Service is defined as the application of resources (e.g., knowledge) for the benefit of another.
  • It is fundamental to social and economic exchange and lies at the core of value co-creation, which aims to make every situation more beneficial and win-win for everyone involved.
  • Service underlies social and economic development, and the wealth of people, businesses, and nations.
  • The service research community aims to understand daily interactions to improve outcomes for people, businesses, and governments, recognizing that great interactions create more value for all stakeholders.
  • Service innovation involves finding new ways for people to apply knowledge for mutually beneficial interactions that improve the quality of life and well-being.
  • It requires the integration of advanced technologies (like AI) for amplifying capabilities, innovative business models for scaling benefits, and effective institutional arrangements for mitigating harms.
  • A key goal of service innovation is to foster improvements in interactions and drive positive change within business and society, while upholding human values (freedom, justice, inclusion), and ensuring sustainability and resilience.
  • Service innovation also involves rebundling resources to create new resources.

  • AI refers to technologies that can act intelligently (like people in some ways) in real-world contexts, often by learning from data or being programmed.
  • AI is rapidly transforming service delivery and consumption, with AI-driven tools and online platforms becoming increasingly prevalent.
  • Technology-mediated service interactions, facilitated by digital platforms, are being reshaped by AI, leading to new opportunities and challenges.
  • Online platforms are defined as a digital service or technology-enabled service that enables interactions between two or more distinct but interdependent sets of users (whether firms or individuals) who interact through the service via the Internet.
  • Service practice involves the responsible application of knowledge and skills to help others, coupled with a commitment to continuous learning (upskilling).
  • It emphasizes value co-creation, collaboration, and ethical considerations in service interactions, all of which are becoming more critical in the AI era.
  • Service professionals must adapt to the evolving service landscape by expanding their skill sets and embracing AI to enhance their capabilities.
  • The International Society of Service Innovation Professionals (ISSIP) supports give-get-grow community engagement at all stages of career development, from student to diverse career roles to retired, to support service innovation.

  • AI is not merely a tool but a transformative force that is reshaping the nature of service interactions, presenting both opportunities and challenges.
  • AI development focuses on both automation (enhancing efficiency and scalability) and augmentation (enhancing human capabilities), both of which have profound implications for service.
  • The integration of AI in service demands a focus on ethical considerations, trust, responsibility, and human-centered design to ensure that AI serves humanity's best interests.
  • Terms like trust, responsibility, co-creation, and ethics are core to the service community and are becoming more important in the AI community.
  • AI-driven automation can streamline routine service tasks, improving efficiency and reducing costs.
  • AI augmentation enhances the capabilities of service providers, enabling them to deliver more personalized, effective, and innovative services.
  • Both automation and augmentation have the potential to improve service quality, accessibility, and scalability, but they also raise important questions about the future of work and the need for upskilling.
  • The automation AI camp envisioned scaling access to complex, knowledge-intensive service, increasing access to the capabilities provided by experts, while the augmentation AI camp envisioned empowering human professionals, allowing them to solve previously unsolvable complex and urgent problems.
